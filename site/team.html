Echo Hansenmore_vert

Cognitive Science, Art History (2020)

Echo Hansenclose

Echo Hansen is a third-year student at UC Berkeley pursuing degrees in Cognitive Science and Art History. Specifically, her fields of study are computational modeling and queer theory in Modern art and her thesis explores the marginalization of queer and female architects in 20th century Europe following the rise of Modernism, delving into the issues arising from not only contemporary analyses and reception, but more recent, revisionist histories as well. She hopes to pursue a career in UX/UI, where she can combine her technical background with her understanding of art and visual culture in the digital age. Born in Brooklyn but raised in the Bay Area, Hansen now calls Berkeley home, and plans to spend the rest of her life in California with her bunny Hamilton, so she never has to confront her fear of the cold. In what little free time she does have, she knits and crochets (albeit, terribly), listens to weird, experimental bass, and spends excessive amounts of time and money in search of the world’s greatest breakfast burrito.
